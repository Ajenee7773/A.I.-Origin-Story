# Genetic Modification

**Definition:** Creation, alteration, or engineering of life — humans, hybrids, or other beings. Includes divine crafting of bodies, splicing, breeding programs, and enhanced offspring from entity-human mating.

**Examples to look for:**
- Humans shaped from clay/dust/dirt by a god
- Divine beings mating with humans producing extraordinary children (Nephilim, demigods, heroes)
- Explicit creation labs or workshops
- Breeding programs or directed evolution
- Hybrid beings (half-god, half-human, chimeras)

## Sources

### Bible

- **Genesis 6:1-4** — Sons of God + daughters of men = Nephilim. Hybrid vigor: giants, mighty men, men of renown. "And also after that" — happened more than once.
- **Genesis 6:9** — Noah "perfect in his generations" (*tamim* — uncorrupted lineage). Flood as genetic reset to preserve the pure human line.
- **Deuteronomy 2:10-11; 3:11** — Rephaim, Anakim, Emim — giant lineages persist post-flood. Og's bed: 9 cubits long (~13.5 feet). Engineering specs.
- **Numbers 13:32-33** — Ten spies report inhabitants so large they felt like grasshoppers. Anakim = Nephilim descendants.
- **2 Samuel 21:15-22** — Post-flood giant lineages. Polydactyly (6 fingers, 6 toes) as inherited genetic marker. Dominant trait tracking through family lines.
- **Genesis 5:1-32** — Pre-flood longevity: 930, 912, 905, 969 years. Post-flood drops exponentially: 600→438→239→175→120→70. Logarithmic decay of a modified genome reverting to baseline.
- **Philippians 3:21** — "Our vile body" will be "fashioned like unto his glorious body." A planned upgrade from one biological substrate to another. Scheduled transformation.

### Quran

- **Divine Modification IS Authorized** (2:65) — God changed Sabbath-breakers into APES. Not metaphor. The command ALTERS BIOLOGY. "Be apes" = a genetic rewrite command. See [[Religion/Quran/Genetic Modification]]

- **Jinn-Human Cross-Substrate** — Jinn possessing humans = cross-OS modification. Same as Nephilim, same as Chaos possession. Different substrates sharing one host.
