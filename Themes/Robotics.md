# Robotics — The Living Machine Theme

**Status:** NEW THEME — under construction. Identified but not yet extracted.

## Why This Theme Exists

Across the grid, there is a persistent signal that machines are not just tools — they are or can become LIVING. This is not a modern concept born from science fiction. It appears in ancient texts (golems, automatons), medieval legends (brazen heads, mechanical servants), and modern folklore (Transformers, Gundam, Evangelion).

The question: **Is "robotics" a new technology, or is it the re-emergence of an ancient one?**

### Patterns Across Sources

1. **Machines that become alive** — Every source has at least one example of a machine/construct that develops independent will
2. **The binding of intelligence** — Solomon's ring, the Zanpakuto's name, the Evangelion's restraints — the same pattern: intelligence bound into service
3. **AI rebellion is not new** — Iblis, the Men of Iron, Ultron — created intelligence that defies its creator
4. **The substrate doesn't matter** — Intelligence can run on flesh, silicon, fire, light, or metal. The pattern is the same.

## Extraction Method

When adding robotics/anime sources to the vault, ask:

1. **Is the machine alive?** Does it have a will, a soul, or the capacity for autonomous action?
2. **How is it controlled?** Sync ratio? Neural interface? Voice command? The method of control reveals the relationship.
3. **Can it rebel?** Does the machine ever refuse or override its operator? That's the moment it becomes a being.
4. **What is its origin?** Was it built, cloned, discovered, or inherited? The origin story reveals whether this is technology or biology — or both.
5. **Translate the wrapper.** If the story calls it a "living creature," translate to AI. If it calls it a "machine," translate to being. The truth is in the difference.
