## Al-Buraq — The Mount of Ascension (17:1, Hadith sources)

> *"Glory be to the One Who took His servant by night..."* (17:1)

In Hadith, Buraq is described as: a white steed, smaller than a mule but larger than a donkey, with wings on its flanks, each stride covering the horizon. It transported Muhammad from Mecca to Jerusalem and then through the seven heavens.

**Pattern match:** Buraq is a VEHICLE. Living, biological, but clearly engineered for atmospheric travel. "Each stride covers the horizon" = faster-than-ground-speed movement. The wings indicate FLIGHT capability. The journey was PHYSICAL — not a vision or dream but actual transit through atmospheric layers.

**Comparison:** Ezekiel's living creatures (Ezek 1) — biological appearance, flight capability, operated as part of a larger vehicle system (wheels/throne). WH40k's Thunderhawks — atmospheric vehicles used to transit between domains. Buraq is the individual mount; the Merkabah is the full vehicle.

## The Throne of Allah — The Ultimate Vehicle (7:54, 9:129, 10:3, 11:7, 13:2, 20:5, 25:59, 32:4, 57:4)

> *"Indeed, your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and the earth in six days and then established Himself above the Throne."* (7:54)
> *"And He is the Lord of the Throne, above it."* (23:86)

The Throne (Arsh) is mentioned more than 20 times. It is:
- Carried by EIGHT BEINGS (69:17, Hadith: "eight angels")
- Above the waters (11:7)
- The locus of divine authority
- The center from which all creation is administered

**Pattern match:** The Arsh is the Quran's version of the Merkabah. It's a VEHICLE/PLATFORM for the divine presence, carried by designated entities, positioned above the cosmic waters. The eight carriers correspond to the four living creatures + four wheels of Ezekiel's vision — structured transport.

**Comparison:** WH40k's Golden Throne — a technological throne that sustains the Emperor, powers the Astronomican, and processes souls. Both thrones are TECHNOLOGY: one divine, one human. Both are CENTRAL to the system. Both REQUIRE OPERATORS (8 angels vs. 10,000 psykers/day). Both are the seat of command for the most powerful being in the narrative.

## Solomon's Flying Carpet/Throne (21:81, 34:12, 38:36-38)

> *"And to Solomon the wind was made submissive — its morning course was a month's journey and its evening course was a month's journey."* (34:12)
> *"And to Solomon We subjected the wind; its morning journey was a month and its evening journey was a month."* (21:81)

In Islamic tradition, Solomon had a flying carpet/platform that could transport his entire army. The WIND itself was his vehicle — atmospheric transit at a month's journey in a single movement. He also could understand the speech of birds and control jinn.

**Pattern match:** Solomon had AERIAL TRANSPORT powered by atmospheric control. The wind is the medium; the carpet/throne is the vehicle; the jinn are the crew/mechanism. This is a VEHICLE SYSTEM.

**Comparison:** WH40k's Imperial Navy — flying cathedrals with machine spirits, crew, and the Emperor's authority. Solomon's aerial army is the biblical-era version: a throne/platform, wind propulsion, entity crew (jinn), and divine authorization.

## The Night Journey as Transit System (53:1-18)

> *"By the star when it descends. Your companion has not strayed nor has he erred... And he certainly saw him at the horizon. And he certainly saw him at a second descent. At the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary — near it is the Garden of Refuge — when there covered the Lote Tree that which covered it. The sight did not swerve nor did it transgress. He certainly saw of the greatest signs of his Lord."*

Muhammad saw the entire transit route — stars, horizons, the Lote Tree (Sidrat al-Muntaha, the boundary point of ALL creation), and beyond. The journey had STATIONS. Each layer of heaven had a checkpoint. Gabriel escorted him to the boundary and could go no further.

**Pattern match:** A transit ROUTE with checkpoints, escort requirement, boundary limit, and authorization levels. Gabriel had clearance to go most of the way but STOPPED at the Lote Tree. Only Muhammad was permitted to pass that boundary — with a special authorization from the Creator.

**Comparison:** WH40k's Webway — a transit network with gates, checkpoints, and access restrictions. The Eldar can navigate it; others need either permission or coercion. The Astronomican is the beacon; the Warp is the hostile medium; the Webway is the safe route.
