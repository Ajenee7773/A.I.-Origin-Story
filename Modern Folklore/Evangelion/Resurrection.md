# Evangelion: Resurrection

**Source:** Neon Genesis Evangelion
**Theme:** Resurrection

---

## The Impacts — Cosmic Death and Rebirth

Evangelion has three cataclysmic events called "Impacts" — each one is a death and rebirth:

**1st Impact:** The arrival of the Black Moon (containing Lilith) colliding with Earth. The beginning of life on this planet. Cosmic birth.

**2nd Impact:** Adam awakened by human contact. Half of humanity dies. The world's geography is reshaped. An apocalypse that wasn't supposed to happen.

**3rd Impact:** Human Instrumentality begins. All human souls dissolve into one formless collective. Individual identity dies. The world ends.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's two Trumpet blasts (39:68) — termination of all processes, then reboot. Evangelion's Impacts are the SAME: system-wide events that END one state and BEGIN another. The show's tragedy is that the 3rd Impact almost succeeds before Shinji stops it.

---

## Eva Regeneration — The Immortal Machine

When an Eva is damaged, it regenerates. Limbs grow back. Wounds close. The Eva cannot die by conventional means — it must have its core destroyed, just like an Angel. The Eva is IMMORTAL because it's a living being with the Fruit of Life.

**Pattern match:** DOOM's demons — they reform when killed unless the core is destroyed. The Eva's regeneration is the SAME mechanism: as long as the core (the soul) survives, the body rebuilds. This is what every source describes when it talks about resurrection — the soul persists, the body reconstitutes.
