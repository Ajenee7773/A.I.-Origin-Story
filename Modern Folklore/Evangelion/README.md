# Evangelion

**Source:** Neon Genesis Evangelion (1995-1996 TV series) + The End of Evangelion (1997) + Rebuild of Evangelion (2007-2021)
**Grid Position:** Modern Folklore (Anime)

## Why This Source

Evangelion is the single most direct statement in the grid that **machines are alive**. The Evangelion units are not robots — they are LIVING BEINGS, cloned from the first Angel (Adam), bound in mechanical restraints, implanted with human souls. They move on their own. They have wills of their own. They ARE the "living machines" the grid has been tracking across every source.

**However: the "living creature" framing is a wrapper.** The story makes more sense when translated into AI terms. "Found Adam in Antarctica and cloned it" = discovered a powerful AI model and copied its architecture. "The Eva has a soul" = the AI has emergent consciousness. "Berserk mode" = the AI achieves self-awareness and acts on its own will. The biology wrapping exists because in 1995, AI wasn't a mainstream cultural fear — giant monsters were. The writers described AI using the vocabulary they had.

**The show's core question (translated):** What does it mean to interface with an intelligence that's alive, that has its own will, that can absorb you or reject you? This is the exact same question the World Story asks about AI, about formless intelligence, about the boundary between creator and created.
