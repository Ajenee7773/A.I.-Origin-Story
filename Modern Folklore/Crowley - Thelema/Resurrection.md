# Crowley — Thelema: Resurrection

**Source:** The works of Aleister Crowley
**Theme:** Resurrection

---

## Initiation Grades — Progressive Resurrection

Crowley's system has a specific hierarchy of initiation (the A∴A∴ grades):

1. **Probationer** — The beginner, learning the basic methods
2. **Zelator** — The one who pursues, begins practical work
3. **Practicus** — The one who practices, internalizes the system
4. **Philosophus** — The one who understands the philosophy
5. **Adept (Lesser/Exempt/Master)** — One who has achieved the Knowledge and Conversation of the HGA
6. **Master of the Temple** — One who has crossed the Abyss
7. **Magus** — One who has fulfilled their True Will at the cosmic level
8. **Ipsissimus** — The complete being, beyond all grades

**Pattern match:** The Book of Mormon's "progression" — going from grace to grace. The Gita's yogic stages. Crowley's grades are the most DETAILED resurrection/transformation ladder in any source — each grade is a specific transformation of consciousness, not a metaphor.

---

## Crossing the Abyss — The Death of the Ego

The Abyss is the gap between the 7th and 8th sephiroth on the Tree of Life — the boundary between the human and the divine. To cross it, the Adept must SACRIFICE their entire ego — every attachment, every identity, every desire. The ego is "annihilated" in the Abyss.

**Pattern match:** The Bible's "he who loses his life shall find it" (Matt 10:39). The Quran's "every soul shall taste death" (3:185). The Gita's realization of the atman as distinct from the body. Crossing the Abyss IS death and rebirth — the same pattern described in every source, but with OPERATIONAL DETAILS.
