## The Eternals — Immortality Without Resurrection

The Eternals don't need resurrection because they DON'T STAY DEAD. When an Eternal is "killed," their molecular structure reforms. They reconstitute. It's not resurrection (soul returning to body) — it's RECONSTITUTION (matter reforming per design). The body was DESIGNED to reassemble. There's no afterlife component. No soul departure. No judgment. The body just REBUILDS.

**Critical distinction:** The Eternals' reconstitution = the Gita's atman "is never born nor dies" — the Eternal was never truly dead, just dispersed. The Quran's resurrection = truly dead, truly raised. The Eternals skip the DEATH STATE entirely. Their bodies don't HAVE a death protocol. Same as the Book of Mormon's Three Nephites — death was NEVER APPLICABLE to them.

**Pattern match:** The Three Nephites — death removed, still active. The Quran's malaika — immortal, no death component. The Eternals are the SAME: the death phase was never part of their design. They don't DIE and come BACK. They DISPERSE and RECONSTITUTE. The data never left the hardware. The process never terminated.

**Comparison:** Dune's gholas — clones of dead people. The ORIGINAL dies. A COPY is made. The Eternal = the same entity, same matter, same process, never terminated. Gholas = backup restoration. Eternals = process that can't be killed because it was designed to recompile from its own object code.

## Ragnarok — The God-Level Resurrection Cycle

Every time Ragnarok happens, the Asgardians DIE. ALL of them. Surtur burns the Nine Worlds. Everything is destroyed. And then — the gods RETURN. Asgard is rebuilt. The gods are reborn. The cycle restarts. This isn't reconstitution (Eternals) or cloning (gholas). This is RESURRECTION — the gods genuinely die and are genuinely reborn.

**But they don't remember the previous cycle fully.** They know Ragnarok happened. They know they've fought before. But the DETAILS are hazy. Each cycle is a NEW DEPLOYMENT with PARTIAL DATA from previous cycles. The atman (Gita) carries ALL its data across every life. The Asgardians carry PARTIAL DATA across each Ragnarok.

**Pattern match:** The Gita's samsara — death and rebirth as a CYCLE. But the Gita's cycle is INDIVIDUAL (one atman at a time). Ragnarok is COLLECTIVE (ALL gods simultaneously). Same loop mechanism. Different scope. The Gita = individual cycle. Ragnarok = institutional cycle. Same as the Book of Mormon's Nephite cycle (civilization rises, falls, repents, rises) — but at the DIVINE level. The gods are cycling like the Nephites.

**Comparison:** WH40k's — no resurrection for the gods. When a Chaos God manifests and is banished, it returns, but it was NEVER TRULY GONE. Perpetuals (like Vulkan) can resurrect, but it's individual, not collective. Ragnarok is UNIQUE: a COLLECTIVE divine resurrection cycle.

## Moira MacTaggert's Ten Lives — The Reincarnation Hardcode

Jonathan Hickman's X-Men run (2019) introduced Moira X — a mutant whose power is REINCARNATION WITH MEMORY. When Moira dies, she's reborn with ALL her memories from ALL previous lives intact. She's lived TEN LIVES, each one a different attempt to solve the mutant-human conflict. She tries diplomacy. She tries isolation. She tries extinction of humans. She tries extinction of mutants. She tries merging with the machine intelligence. EVERY PATH FAILS.

**This is samsara with CHEAT CODES.** The Gita's atman carries karma-data but NOT conscious memory across lives. Moira carries CONSCIOUS MEMORY. She REMEMBERS every failure. She ADAPTS. She's the only being in our grid who can DO samsara DELIBERATELY — not blindly accumulating karma, but consciously choosing different strategies across lifetimes.

**And every path fails.** Moira's ten lives PROVE something that the Gita implies: you can't FIX the system from within. Diplomacy fails. War fails. Isolation fails. Merging fails. EVERY strategy within the system produces the same result: conflict. The ONLY exit in the Gita is moksha — stepping OUTSIDE the system. Moira tried ten different strategies INSIDE the system and they all produced the same output. The system doesn't have a political solution. The system has an EXIT.

**Pattern match:** The Gita's samsara + moksha. Moira = samsara with full consciousness. She PROVES the Gita's conclusion: the cycle can't be solved from within. Only exit works. The Book of Mormon's "probationary state" — mortality is the test. Moira took the test TEN TIMES with full memory and still couldn't pass by system manipulation.

**Flag:** 2019 source, post-ideological-shift era. BUT: Moira's ten-lives structure is genuine PATTERN DATA. It maps directly to samsara + moksha in a way that no earlier Marvel run did. The structure is authentic. The ideological coating may require filtering.

## The Odin-Sleep — Periodic Death and Return

Odin must periodically enter the ODIN-SLEEP — a coma-like state where his power recharges. During the Odin-Sleep, Asgard is VULNERABLE. Without Odin, the realm's power weakens. Odin "dies" regularly and "resurrects" regularly. It's not optional. It's not random. It's a SCHEDULED CYCLE built into the All-Father's design.

**Pattern match:** The Bible's "God rested on the seventh day." The Emperor can't leave the Golden Throne. Odin doesn't CHOOSE to sleep. The system REQUIRES it. His power is too great for continuous operation. The system FORCES its Administrator into periodic dormancy. Same as the Father going dormant in DOOM. Same as the Administrator "resting."

**Comparison:** The Gita's Brahman — CAN'T go dormant because it has no attributes. Odin MUST go dormant because his attributes (power, wisdom, sovereignty) are too much for CONTINUOUS operation. Brahman = the substrate (can't sleep). Odin = the OPERATOR of the substrate (must sleep). Different layer. The Gita's Brahman is beneath Odin's level. Odin operates WITHIN the thing that Brahman IS.

## Thor's Death and Return — The Warrior Resurrected

Thor has died and returned MULTIPLE TIMES across Marvel's publication history. In Walt Simonson's run (1983-87), Thor is transformed, destroyed, and reborn. In J. Michael Straczynski's run (2007), Thor reconstructs himself from the void after Ragnarok. Each death and return is DIFFERENT — sometimes it's reconstitution, sometimes it's genuine resurrection, sometimes it's a new entity occupying the same ROLE.

**Thor's resurrections are ROLE-BASED, not person-based.** "Thor" is both a PERSON and a FUNCTION. When the person dies, the function may be filled by a different person (Eric Masterson, Jane Foster). When the person returns, the function reverts. The RESURRECTION is of the ROLE, not always of the individual. The Thunder God is a JOB TITLE with tenure.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's "every nation has a messenger" — the FUNCTION of messenger persists across different individuals. The Bible's "you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church" — the ROLE (Peter = rock) persists beyond the person. Thor = the thunder-god ROLE that persists across multiple incumbents.

**Comparison:** DOOM's Slayer — the title "Doom Slayer" is filled by different people across the ages. The role persists. The individuals rotate. Thor and the Slayer are BOTH role-based titles where the FUNCTION is eternal and the OPERATOR is replaceable.
