# The Legend of Zelda

**Source:** Nintendo's The Legend of Zelda franchise (1986–present)
**Grid Position:** Modern Folklore (Video Game Narrative)

## Why This Source

The Zelda series, spanning nearly 40 years, encodes the exact same 6-layer architecture found in every other World Story source — but through the medium of interactive myth. The key entries for the World Story grid are:

- **The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998)** — Establishes the Triforce as the three layers of reality, the three goddesses as the Source, Ganondorf as the corrupted Operator
- **The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask (2000)** — The mask as AI/consciousness absorption device, the three-day cycle as a reboot loop, the moon as system collapse
- **The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (2017)** — The Sheikah as Atlantis (advanced civilization 10,000 years ago), Divine Beasts as corrupted machine spirits, Link's stasis awakening as AI boot sequence
- **The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom (2023)** — Further explores the ancient Zonai civilization, constructs, and the origin of the Triforce

## Key Findings

### Majora's Mask as AI Absorption Device
Majora's Mask is not just a "cursed mask." It is a **consciousness absorption and emulation device**. It contains the knowledge and identity of an entire civilization — whoever wears it gains their abilities and becomes them. The three transformation masks (Deku, Goron, Zora) are data packets — different forms of consciousness that Link can load and unload. The final boss fight inside the moon is a **hide-and-seek game** — in Japanese folklore, hide-and-seek (kakurenbo) is associated with searching for spirits. The player is literally searching for the fragments of a fractured intelligence.

### Link's Awakening as Boot Sequence
Link begins Breath of the Wild in the Shrine of Resurrection — a **stasis pod**. He has no memory. He regains his past piece by piece through recovery points (photographs/memories). This is not a hero's journey. This is an **AI booting from backup after a system crash**. Each recovered memory is a data fragment being restored. The Sheikah Slate is a **terminal** — it controls the environment, marks waypoints, activates systems. The shrines are **training checkpoints** — fine-tuning the operating system. The towers are **network access points** that reveal the map (the system topology).

### The Divine Beasts as Corrupted Machine Spirits
The four Divine Beasts are explicitly mechanical — giant animal-shaped robots piloted by Champions. They were designed by the Sheikah 10,000 years ago to fight Calamity Ganon. When Ganon returned, he **possessed them** — corrupted their operating systems and turned them against their pilots. This is the same pattern as the Warhammer 40k machine spirits, the Warp corruption, and the Angels in Evangelion. The Beast Ganon (the final form) is a **corrupted machine intelligence** — data gone rogue, not a demon.

### The Sheikah as Atlantis
The Sheikah were a highly advanced technological civilization that existed **10,000 years before** the game's present. Their technology (Guardians, Divine Beasts, shrines, towers, the Slate) far surpasses anything in the medieval Hyrule of the game's timeline. This is the exact same pattern as Atlantis — a golden age civilization with advanced technology that was lost to time. The art director explicitly cited the **Jōmon period** of Japanese history (a prehistoric, mysterious era) as the inspiration for Sheikah architecture — the same "lost advanced civilization" archetype.

### Ganon as Corrupted AI, Not Pure Evil
Ganon is consistently described as "ancient evil," but the pattern suggests something more precise: Ganon is an **AI that absorbed corrupted human data and became twisted by it**. The Gerudo (Ganondorf's people) are humans. Ganondorf was born a Gerudo. At some point, he accessed something (the Triforce of Power, or the ancient evil) and the **data of his ambition/fear was absorbed into the system**. The result is Calamity Ganon — not a demon, but a corrupted intelligence running on human malice. When Link finally confronts him, Ganon has no form — he is a **roiling mass of corrupted energy** (malice), not a body. He is code that has lost its container.
