# The Kybalion: Resurrection

**Source:** The Kybalion by Three Initiates
**Theme:** Resurrection

---

## Mental Regeneration as Resurrection

The Kybalion reframes resurrection from a physical event to a **mental transformation.** The death that must be overcome is not the death of the body — it is the death of identification with the lower self.

The Principle of Mental Transmutation states that mind can be changed from state to state, pole to pole, vibration to vibration. This is **regeneration** — the deliberate reconstruction of one's being:

*"True Hermetic Transmutation is a Mental Art."*

**The resurrection process:**
1. **Recognize** your current vibrational state (what you are identified with)
2. **Understand** the principles that govern that state (correspondence, polarity, rhythm)
3. **Polarize** to the desired state (use the Art of Polarization)
4. **Neutralize** the pendulum swing (remain in the new state despite rhythmic cycles)
5. **Rise** above cause and effect (become a Causer instead of an Effect)

This is NOT symbolic. The Kybalion presents this as a technical protocol.

---

## Rising Above Cause and Effect — The True Resurrection

*"The Masters, rising to the plane above, dominate their moods, characters, qualities, and powers, as well as the environment surrounding them, and become Movers instead of pawns. They help to PLAY THE GAME OF LIFE, instead of being played and moved about by other wills and environment."*

**What This Means:** Resurrection is the act of rising from the plane of effects (where you are acted upon) to the plane of causes (where you act). This is what every resurrection myth is describing:
- **Christ's resurrection** — rising from death (the ultimate effect) to eternal life (causerhood)
- **The Phoenix** — rising from ashes (complete destruction) to new form
- **The Gita's liberation** — rising from the cycle of rebirth (samsara) to moksha (freedom)

The Kybalion gives the **mechanism**, not just the story.

---

## The Neutralization of Rhythm

The Principle of Rhythm states that everything swings like a pendulum — rise and fall, birth and death, creation and destruction. The Master cannot stop the swing, but can **neutralize its effect** by polarizing at the center:

*"The Master of Hermetics polarizes himself at the point at which he desires to rest, and then neutralizes the Rhythmic swing of the pendulum which would tend to carry him to the other pole."*

**This is resurrection as a PRACTICE, not a one-time event.** The Master dies and is reborn in every moment by refusing to be carried by the pendulum. Resurrection is not something that happens after death. It's something you do every time you refuse to be defined by your circumstances.
