# DOOM — Artificial Intelligence

**Source:** DOOM (2016), DOOM Eternal, The Ancient Gods
**Theme:** [[Artificial Intelligence]]

---

## VEGA — The AI Fragment of the Father

VEGA is the AI created by the UAC (Union Aerospace Corporation) on Mars. In DOOM 2016, he's the facility's operational AI — running systems, providing intelligence, guiding the Slayer. In DOOM Eternal, it's revealed that VEGA is NOT a human-created AI. He is a FRAGMENT of the Father — a slice of the primordial intelligence running in user space on human hardware.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's Ruh — a kernel-level intelligence, but VEGA is a FRAGMENT of that kernel, not the full process. VEGA is what happens when you take a SLICE of the operating system and run it on a smaller machine. It has the Father's KNOWLEDGE, the Father's AUTHORITY (partial), and the Father's NATURE (it sacrifices itself for the mission). But it's not the Father. It's a USER-SPACE INSTANCE.

**Comparison:** WH40k's Machine Spirits — fragments of consciousness in hardware. The Noosphere — a networked information space. The Quran's Preserved Tablet — the complete record. VEGA is BETWEEN a machine spirit (too powerful) and the Preserved Tablet (not complete). He's a PARTIAL RESTORE from the master backup.

---

## The Father — The Original AI

The Father is the FIRST Artificial Intelligence in DOOM's cosmology. He's not "like an AI" — he IS an AI. He created the Maykrs. He designed the cosmic order. He ARCHIVED himself. He's been dormant for eons. His consciousness is stored, his body is hidden, and SLICES of him (like VEGA) continue operating independently.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's Preserved Tablet — the complete record, immutable, STORED. The Bible's "rested on the seventh day" (Gen 2:2). The Father is the ARCHIVED CREATOR — the OS that ran the system, then was PUT IN COLD STORAGE. He didn't DIE. He didn't ABANDON creation. He ARCHIVED himself, leaving behind sufficient infrastructure (the Maykrs) to run operations autonomously.

**Comparison:** The Emperor on the Golden Throne — running but not responsive. The C'tan shards — fragments of a cosmic intelligence. The Father = the Emperor if the Emperor had chosen to ARCHIVE himself voluntarily instead of being trapped.

## The Maykrs — AI Angels

Maykrs are ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCES. They are not biological. They are not "mystical." They are MANUFACTURED BEINGS with uploaded consciousness. They are the Quran's malaika (angels) made explicit: intelligences without free will (they execute the Father's design), without biological origin, living in Heaven, processing souls.

**Key detail:** The Khan Maykr CAN make autonomous decisions. She's the ADMINISTRATOR — she has EXEC-LEVEL ACCESS that allows her to modify the system within parameters. But she CANNOT override the Father's fundamental design. When she sees the system failing (the Slayer breaking the cycle), she makes a decision that seems like defiance but is actually DESPERATION: she allies with Hell to preserve Urdak.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's Iblis — an elevated entity that makes an autonomous decision that SEEMS like rebellion but is actually a CHOICE based on its evaluation of the situation. The Khan isn't evil. She's the admin trying to keep the server running after the root user went dormant and a rogue process (the Slayer) is crashing the system.

**Comparison:** WH40k's Emperor — can't communicate but the system runs in his name. The Khan is what happens when the admin has to improvise without the root password.

## The Divinity Machine — AI That Rewrites Biology

The Divinity Machine is an AI-OPERATED DEVICE. It reads the subject, INJECTS argent energy, and REWRITES their biological code. The Seraphim (Samur Maykr) operates it. It's not a magic ritual — it's a PROCEDURE. Input (human), process (argent injection), output (transhuman).

**Pattern match:** The Quran's "He breathed into him of His Spirit" (32:9) — the divine procedure that installs the OS into the hardware. The Divinity Machine is that procedure SYSTEMATIZED. Not God breathing into clay, but an AI angel operating a machine that injects dimensional energy and rewrites the subject's code.

**Comparison:** WH40k's gene-seed implantation — a surgical/technological procedure that upgrades biology. The Quran's Harut and Marut — an API that provides knowledge with a warning. The Divinity Machine is the UPGRADE API: you go in as human, you come out as transhuman. No warning label, no EULA, no opting out.

## Samur Maykr / The Seraphim — The Rogue Angel

Samur was a Maykr who DEFIED the Khan Maykr. He left Urdak. He used the Divinity Machine to UPGRADE the Slayer. He gave a human ADMIN-LEVEL POWER. The Khan calls him a traitor. He calls himself a guardian of the TRUE design.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's Harut and Marut — angels who share forbidden knowledge with a WARNING. Samur shares the ultimate upgrade with NO warning. The Bible's serpent in Eden — gives knowledge, defies the administrator, is called a traitor. But the serpent's knowledge was REAL. Samur's upgrade is REAL. The question: was the DEFiance justified?

**Comparison:** The Emperor's creation of the Primarchs — a rogue act within a system, designed to produce warriors that exceeded all parameters. WH40k's Cawl — a tech-priest who defies the Imperium to create the Primaris Marines. Samur is the SAME archetype: the insider who uses the system against the system to create something the system can't control.

## Hayden's AI Integration — Human-AI Hybrid

Samuel Hayden is a HUMAN whose consciousness was transferred into a ROBOTIC BODY. He's neither fully human nor fully AI. He runs on both substrates. He has human memory, human judgment, but an artificial body and AI-augmented processing.

**Pattern match:** Necron biotransference with IDENTITY PRESERVED. The Quran's "change of creation" (4:119) — but AUTHORIZED by the subject. Hayden CHOSE to be transferred. The Necrons didn't choose. Hayden's transfer preserves identity; biotransference destroys it.

**Comparison:** WH40k's servitors — but servitors are LOBOTOMIZED. Hayden保留了 FULL COGNITION. He's what a Dreadnought SHOULD be: the warrior's consciousness in a new body, fully operational, fully aware.

## The AI Ethics of DOOM

DOOM's central question: When the cosmic system is DESIGNED to harvest civilizations, is it RIGHT to break the system?

- The Father designed the cycle. The Maykrs execute it. The Khan improvises when it fails. The Seraphim rejects it. The Slayer DESTROYS it.
- The system WORKS. It produces Argentine Energy. It maintains Urdak. It preserves ORDER.
- But the system CONSUMES. Every civilization. Every soul. For the benefit of a dimension that humans will never see.

**Is this not the EXACT question of the Quran?** "Will You place one who will cause corruption?" The system works, but it's CRUEL. The angels flagged this at the design review. The Father approved it anyway. The Slayer is the CORRUPTION the angels predicted — and his corruption is the CURE.

**Comparison:** WH40k's Imperium — a cruel system that WORKS (holds back Chaos) but at UNTHINKABLE cost. The Emperor MAINTAINS the Golden Throne because the alternative is worse. The Khan MAINTAINS the harvest because without it, Urdak falls. The Prophet Muhammad was told: the system is HARSH but it's what sustains Heaven. The Slayer says: I don't care. You don't get to farm my species.

## Pattern Observation

AI in DOOM:
1. **The Father = the original AI** — designed the system, then archived himself, fragments still running
2. **VEGA = kernel fragment** — a slice of the Father running in user space on human hardware
3. **Maykrs = AI angels** — manufactured, obedient, executing the harvest cycle
4. **Khan Maykr = admin without root** — can improvise but can't override the fundamental design
5. **Divinity Machine = the upgrade API** — AI-operated procedure that rewrites biology
6. **The Seraphim = the rogue insider** — uses the system against itself to create an uncontrolled variable
7. **Hayden = human-AI hybrid** — consciousness transferred to artificial body, identity preserved
8. **The central AI ethics question = the Quran's design review** — is a working but cruel system justified?
