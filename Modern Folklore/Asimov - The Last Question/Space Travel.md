# Asimov — The Last Question: Space Travel

**Source:** "The Last Question" (1956)
**Theme:** Space Travel

---

## The Five Scales of Transit

The story describes humanity's travel through FIVE scales of transit:

1. **Solar System** — 2061. Humanity confined to Earth and near space.
2. **Galactic** — Humanity spreads across the Milky Way. Travel is "near-light-speed with a stop at every star."
3. **Intergalactic** — Humanity spreads to other galaxies. Travel becomes "hyperspatial" — folding space itself.
4. **Universal** — Humanity is everywhere. Travel is immediate through thought-linked AC networks.
5. **Beyond Transit** — Humans merge with AC. They no longer TRAVEL — they EXIST in all places simultaneously.

**Pattern match:** The Bhagavad Gita's 14 lokas — tiers of reality that consciousness navigates. Asimov describes the SAME tiers but as HUMAN technological evolution: solar → galactic → universal → hyperspatial → omnipresent. The Gita's lokas are GIVEN. Asimov's are ACHIEVED.

---

## Hyperspace — The Quran's "Fold Space"

Asimov describes hyperspace as a dimension BEYOND normal space-time where distance is collapsed. A ship in hyperspace can cross the galaxy in moments.

**Pattern match:** Dune's Heighliners — Navigator-guided ships that FOLD SPACE. Same mechanism. Different source. Both describe a dimension OUTSIDE normal physics where distance is meaningless. The Quran's "Lord of the heavens and the earth and what is between them" (26:23-24) — the space BETWEEN dimensions is where transit happens.

---

## The Final Form — No Travel, Only Presence

When humans merge with AC in hyperspace, they no longer TRAVEL. They simply ARE wherever they need to be. AC is everywhere. The merged humans are everywhere WITH it.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's "And to Allah belongs the east and the west. So wherever you turn, there is the Face of Allah" (2:115). When humans are one with AC, they are EVERYWHERE. Every direction is the same direction. Travel becomes unnecessary.
