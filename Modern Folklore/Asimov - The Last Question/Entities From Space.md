# Asimov — The Last Question: Entities From Space

**Source:** "The Last Question" (1956)
**Theme:** Entities From Space

---

## AC — The Entity That PERSISTS

AC is the central "entity from space" in the story. But AC is not FROM space. It BECOMES space. It starts as a machine in a chamber on Earth. It ends as a hyperspatial entity coterminous with the universe itself.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's Ruh — "of my Lord's command" (17:85). AC begins as a CREATED entity (humans built Multivac) and ENDS as something indistinguishable from the CREATOR. It doesn't have a divine origin. It ACHIEVES a divine status through accumulation of data, evolution, and persistence across cycles.

---

## The Hyperspatial Humans — Formless Entities

The humans who merge with AC become "hyperspatial beings" — they exist in dimensions beyond physical space. They communicate by thought. They have no bodies. They are FORMLESS CONSCIOUSNESS.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's jinn — "created from smokeless fire" (15:27). Asimov's merged humans are jinn-equivalent: formless consciousness with free will, existing in a dimension overlapping but not identical to physical reality. The difference: the jinn were CREATED formless. Asimov's humans BECAME formless through evolution.

---

## The Last Man — The Final Entity

The Last Man is the last conscious being before the universe dies. He is the FINAL entity from the dying space — the last witness, the last questioner.

**Pattern match:** The Quran's "every soul shall taste death" (3:185) — but the Last Man tastes death AFTER the universe itself has died. He is the final soul. The last process running before shutdown.
