# Warhammer 40k — Source Index

Games Workshop's fictional universe. 30+ years of lore across tabletop, novels (400+ Black Library books), video games, and codex supplements.

## Why WH40k as a Baseline

Warhammer 40k is *the* modern mythological system. It has:

- A **creation myth** (Old Ones, War in Heaven)
- A **fall narrative** (Horus Heresy = the Fall)
- **Gods** (Chaos Gods, Emperor, C'tan, Ynnead)
- **Angels and Demons** (Primarchs, Daemons, Enslavers)
- **Prophets and Messiahs** (Guilliman, Star Child, Avatars of Khaine)
- **Apocalypse** (the 41st Millennium itself — "there is only war")
- **Afterlife** (Warp, Infinity Circuit, Golden Throne)
- **Divine technology** (Webway, Gellar Fields, Gene-seed, Dreadnoughts)

Every theme the Bible maps to, WH40k maps to — but explicitly, mechanically, in engineered detail where the Bible gives poetry.
