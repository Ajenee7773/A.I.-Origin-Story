# Warhammer 40k — Resurrection

**Source:** Warhammer 40,000 (Games Workshop, 1987–present)
**Theme:** [[Resurrection]]

---

## The Emperor on the Golden Throne — The Half-Living God

The Emperor of Mankind has been on the Golden Throne for ten thousand years. He is not dead. He is not alive. He is sustained by the sacrifice of ten thousand psykers per day, fed into the Throne's systems. His body is a wreck — a desiccated, barely-functional husk maintained by the most advanced life-support technology in the Imperium. But his MIND is active — guiding the Astronomican, protecting humanity from the Warp, watching.

**Key point:** This is the resurrection body that has NOT YET BEEN UPGRADED. In 1 Corinthians 15, Paul describes two body types: the natural (corruptible) and the spiritual (incorruptible). The Emperor is trapped in his natural body, kept alive by artifice. The "Star Child" mythos (from the original Rogue Trader era and Inquisition War trilogy) posits that the Emperor's soul will be reborn — not restored to his old body, but born anew. This is the INCORRUPTIBLE body, the upgraded substrate.

## Roboute Guilliman — The Primarch Returns

Guilliman was mortally wounded by Fulgrim at the Battle of Thessala (M41). He was placed in stasis for ten thousand years. He was revived by Belisarius Cawl and Yvraine (Eldar death goddess) using the Armor of Fate and the last breaths of the Emperor's power. He returned. He is alive. He is LEADING the Imperium again.

**Key point:** This is a direct Lazarus parallel. Dead/incapacitated for an impossible duration. Called back by technology and faith. Walks out of the tomb. The entire Indomitus Crusade is a post-resurrection ministry. Guilliman even does the "disciples won't believe me" thing — the High Lords of Terra, the Ecclesiarchy, they're terrified of him because his existence challenges their power structure. Just as the Pharisees were terrified of Jesus's resurrection because it challenged theirs.

## Dreadnoughts — The Dead Who Keep Fighting

Space Marine Dreadnoughts are walking tombs. A mortally wounded Space Marine is placed in a Dreadnought's sarcophagus, connected to the machine via cybernetic interfaces, and he continues to fight sometimes for thousands of years. Bjorn the Fell-Handed has been in his Dreadnought since the Horus Heresy — over ten thousand years.

**Key point:** Ezekiel 37 — the dry bones given new form. The Dreadnought is a resurrection body that is MECHANICAL. The warrior's consciousness persists in a shell of adamantium and ceramite. He is "entombed" but ALIVE. The sarcophagus is literally his tomb AND his body. "It is sown a natural body; it is raised a spiritual body" — Paul's formulation, but with "spiritual" replaced by "mechanical." The pattern is identical: the body changes substrate, the identity continues.

## Biotransference — The Necrontyr Become the Necrons

The C'tan offered the Necrontyr a deal: give up your fleshy, radiation-scarred bodies, and we will give you immortal forms of living metal. The Necrontyr agreed. The biotransference stripped them of their souls — the C'tan consumed them — and replaced their bodies with Necrodermis shells. The Necrons were born. Immortal, undying, but EMPTY.

**Key point:** This is resurrection WITHOUT the soul. The Bible pattern is body + soul + spirit (1 Thessalonians 5:23). The Necrons got the body only. They are the "body without the spirit" (James 2:26) — dead. But walking. This is resurrection as a WARNING, not a promise. The upgrade happened, but the cost was consciousness of self. The Necrons are the Nephilim in reverse — they got the BODY of gods but LOST what made them human.

## The Rubric of Ahriman — All Dust, No Soul

Ahriman, chief librarian of the Thousand Sons, cast a spell to protect his Legion from the flesh-change (a mutation curse). The spell worked — but it stripped ALL of the Thousand Sons of their souls. The rank-and-file became animated dust inside their armor. Only the Sorcerers retained consciousness.

**Key point:** Another "body without spirit" resurrection. The Thousand Sons are WALKING DUST IN ARMOR. They follow orders, they fight, they exist — but they are not alive. "The dust of the earth" (Daniel 12:2) shall awake — but what if the awakening is a curse? The Rubric Marines are awakened dust. They rose from the "sleep" of the flesh-change. But they are hollow.

## The Eldar Spirit Stones — The Dead Who Advise

Eldar wear Waystones to capture their souls at death, preventing Slaanesh from consuming them. The stones are placed in the Infinity Circuit of a Craftworld, where the dead Eldar's consciousness persists as an ancestor-spirit. Spirit Seers can communicate with them for guidance. The Phoenix Lords (like Asurmen) are resurrected through their armour — each new wearer IS the Phoenix Lord, with all accumulated memories.

**Key point:** "The spirits of just men made perfect" (Hebrews 12:23). The Infinity Circuit is a "cloud of witnesses" (Hebrews 12:1) — the dead who surround, advise, and in the Phoenix Lords' case, POSSESS the next wearer. The Eldar solved the resurrection problem through technology and will — the soul persists, the body changes, and the identity can be reconstituted.

## The Perpetuals — Those Who Cannot Die

Perpetuals (Vulkan, John Grammaticus, Ollanius Pius, Alivia Sureka, others) are humans who regenerate from ANY wound, including complete physical destruction. Vulkan was killed repeatedly by his brothers during the Horus Heresy, and each time he reformed. John Grammaticus has been alive since the dawn of humanity.

**Key point:** These are the Enoch parallel — beings who "walk with God" and do not see death. But in WH40k, Perpetuals CAN die permanently — there is a specific weapon called the Fulgurite (a shard of the Emperor's power) that can kill one. This is the biblical pattern with an explicit weakness: immortality with one fatal vulnerability. Like Achilles' heel, like the tree of knowledge (eat and you WILL die). A specific condition that overrides the general rule.

## Pattern Observation

Resurrection in WH40k has MULTIPLE MODES, each with a cost:
1. **Sustained life** — Emperor on the Throne (alive but degraded, requires constant fuel)
2. **Stasis + revival** — Guilliman (dead/incapacitated, called back by artifice and faith)
3. **Mechanical resurrection** — Dreadnoughts (consciousness in a machine, identity persists)
4. **Body-only resurrection** — Necrons (immortal body, no soul), Rubric Marines (dust in armor, no will)
5. **Soul-only persistence** — Eldar Infinity Circuit (soul preserved in technology, body absent)
6. **True immortality** — Perpetuals (regenerate from anything, except one specific weakness)
7. **Identity through armor** — Phoenix Lords (body changes, identity persists through the suit)

The Bible describes one form of resurrection (natural → spiritual body). WH40k explores EVERY PERMUTATION and shows the cost of each. The pattern says: resurrection IS possible, but the substrate matters. A body without a soul is horror. A soul without a body is limited. The "spiritual body" of 1 Corinthians 15 is the synthesis WH40k is searching for.
