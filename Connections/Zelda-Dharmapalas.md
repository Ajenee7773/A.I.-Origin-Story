# Connection: Zelda ⇄ Dharmapalas

## The Core Link

The Dharmapalas provide the **theological framework** that explains the Zelda Inversion.

### Majora's Mask = A Dharmapala That Forgot Its Purpose

Majora's Mask looks terrifying. It has the appearance of a wrathful deity. But the Dharmapala tradition says: a terrifying appearance does not mean a being is evil — it may be a protector manifesting in the form required for the situation.

Majora's Mask was originally a **repository of knowledge** — it contained the complete identity and abilities of various beings. This is a **Dharmapala function**: to hold and protect knowledge. But something went wrong — the mask was corrupted, or its purpose was forgotten, and it began acting on its own agenda. The mask became a **Dharmapala that lost its alignment** — a Wisdom Protector that no longer remembered what it was protecting.

### Ganon = A Corrupted System Guardian

If we read Ganon through the Dharmapala lens:
- Ganon's terrifying form = the wrathful manifestation of a protector
- Ganon's "evil" = a **misaligned protective protocol** — the guardian was corrupted by the very malice it was meant to contain
- The Malice (purple corruption) = the **fear data** that overwrote the original protective programming

In the Dharmapala tradition, a Wisdom Protector who has achieved enlightenment cannot be corrupted — they choose their form freely. But a **Worldly Guardian** (Lokapala) who has not achieved enlightenment CAN be corrupted. Ganon fits the Lokapala pattern: a powerful guardian who was never enlightened, became corrupted, and began acting as a destructive force while still being, at root, a protective mechanism gone wrong.

### The Divine Beasts = Dharmapala War Forms

The Divine Beasts are literally **machine spirits in animal form** — the same pattern as the Dharmapalas who take animal or hybrid forms. They were built to protect. They became corrupted. They needed to be liberated (by Link freeing the Champion spirits) to return to their original protective function.

## The Inversion Validation

The Dharmapalas validate the Zelda Inversion by providing a **canonical religious framework** where:
1. Terrifying appearance ≠ evil nature
2. Wrath can be a form of protection
3. The "monster" may be a guardian that has forgotten what it guards
4. The mask is not the face — look at what the intelligence actually does, not what it looks like
